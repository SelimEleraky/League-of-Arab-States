About the League of Arab States

The League of Arab States is a regional organization that brings together Arab nations in a shared effort to advance cooperation, promote development, and strengthen collective stability across the Middle East and North Africa.

A regional institution rooted in cooperation

Founded in Cairo in 1945, the League of Arab States was established to strengthen the bonds between Arab countries and to provide a framework for joint action on matters of regional importance. From its earliest years, the organization has worked to support diplomacy, encourage economic collaboration, and preserve the shared interests of its member states.

Today, the League continues to serve as an important platform for dialogue and coordination, addressing issues that span politics, development, culture, social welfare, and regional security. Its work reflects a long-term commitment to unity, resilience, and constructive engagement.

Our role in the Arab world

The League plays a vital role in helping member states respond to shared challenges and seize common opportunities. Through dialogue, cooperation, and coordinated policy efforts, it supports initiatives that strengthen institutions and improve the quality of life across the region.

  • Promoting dialogue among member states
  • Supporting regional development and stability
  • Encouraging cultural and social exchange
